Guided Meditation Imagery

Below are example exercises that I sometimes use in sessions to help clarify and focus the mind. The audio below can be used by anyone, but in session these exercises are often custom-tailored to you as an individual. This is a tool to assist in ones awareness, insight and find peace and serenity. The offerings here are based in Buddhist Vipassana meditation. Other words to describe these are: Creative visualization, learning mindfulness, prayer and hypnosis.

Tensing and relaxing

This first tensing and relaxing exercise is a simple introduction to meditative practice. The focus is on the physical body and the breath. It is a good place to start.

The Relaxing Cloud

The second exercise creates a simple visualization to focus your mind and attention. It was chosen to allow an exploration of your inner dimension, or your inner self.

The Breathing Exercise

This is a hypnotic exercise that allows you to develop a deeper meditative process. It is also centering on the physical body and the breath as bringing you a deeper into a suggestive process to manage troubling thoughts and begin to transform them into something more positive.

Clarifying with Mother Earth

This first tensing and relaxing exercise is a simple introduction to meditative practice. The focus is on the physical body and the breath. It is a good place to start.

Eating Mindfully

This meditation is a fun yet dramatic way to understand that “mindfulness” is not hard. You will need a few raisons for the best results! On the serious side, this exercise can be very helpful to those who struggle with food, food addictions and weight. It also illustrates that meditation does not take years of practice, or to be a Zen Master to create a benefit. Try it.

Inner Sanctuary

This meditation asks you to find a special, safe place inside. It is for some one who has some experience with meditation and the induction process. By developing the suggestion here you can use this sanctuary both in meditation and in your conscious self. It can be used to learn more deeply about oneself.

Inner Guide

This meditation is to be used with your “inner sanctuary” and with some experience with meditation and the induction process. As you develop this imagery you are challenged to create a wise inner self that can then be tapped to assist your own development.
